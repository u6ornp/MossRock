What Matters

Factors that shape your payment

Four key elements every homebuyer should understand

🏠

Home Price & Loan Amount

Purchase price minus down payment equals your loan. Higher loan = higher monthly payments and more total interest.

📅

Loan Term

30-year = lower monthly payment but more total interest. 15-year = higher payment but dramatically less interest paid overall.

📈

Interest Rate

Even 0.5% difference can mean tens of thousands over a loan's life. Driven by credit score, down payment, and market conditions.

💰

Down Payment

20%+ avoids PMI, reduces loan amount, and often earns a better rate. It also builds immediate home equity.

The 28/36 Rule of Thumb

Housing costs should stay under 28% of gross monthly income. Total debt under 36%. Use this as your affordability guardrail.

Understanding Your Schedule

Why amortization matters

1

Early payments are mostly interest

In the first years of a mortgage, the vast majority of each payment goes toward interest, not reducing your balance. This is "front-loaded" amortization.

2

Extra payments have a big impact

Even $100/month extra can shave years off your loan and save thousands in interest. Use the extra payment field to see the difference instantly.

3

Equity builds slowly at first

Because early payments are interest-heavy, your equity grows slowly in the first years. Refinancing or selling early may mean you've paid more interest than principal.

Refinancing Guide

When does refinancing make sense?

1

Rate drops by 1%+

A common rule of thumb: refinancing is worth considering when your new rate is at least 1% lower than your current rate. Even 0.5% can make sense depending on your loan size.

2

You plan to stay long enough

Closing costs typically run 2–5% of the loan amount. Make sure you'll stay in the home long enough after the break-even point to capture the monthly savings.

3

Your credit has improved

A better credit score since origination may qualify you for a lower rate even without a market rate change. A 50-point improvement can meaningfully lower your rate.

FAQ

Common questions

A typical mortgage payment includes PITI: Principal (reduces your balance), Interest (cost of borrowing), Taxes (property taxes in escrow), and Insurance (homeowner's insurance and PMI if your down payment is under 20%).

A 15-year mortgage carries a lower rate and you pay 40–60% less total interest, but monthly payments are significantly higher. A 30-year loan offers cash flow flexibility at the cost of more total interest paid.

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is below 20%. It costs ~0.5–1.5% of the loan per year. Avoid it by putting 20%+ down, or use a VA/USDA loan which don't require PMI.

Refinancing makes sense when your new rate is meaningfully lower (typically 0.5–1%+), you plan to stay in the home past your break-even point, and closing costs are reasonable relative to monthly savings. Use our Refinance Calculator to find your exact break-even.

Amortization is the process of spreading loan payments over time. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal. Understanding your amortization schedule helps you see when you build real equity and how extra payments accelerate payoff.
